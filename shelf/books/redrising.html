red rising - pierce brown

dates: [08.30 ~ 09.12.24]

rating: [**---]

I would have probably ate this shit up back in high school, when I was oblivious to my future and what I suffer now. This book aims to entertain you, but I couldn’t shake the feeling I was being sold a fantasy out of my own suffering and hopes - late stage capitalism; Americas boot on my neck 5 days a week.

The communist iconography and symbolism is impossible to avoid: The bottom class are called “reds”. A sling blade being chosen as Darrows symbol to his meteoric rise against the bourgeois class of “golds”. This book invites you to escape and fantasize about rising up. Usually I can fuck with that if it wasn’t so escapist in nature. IMO it just leaves a sour taste in my mouth. I get that’s maybe exclusive to me, but hey. That’s how I felt when I read it! In summary, this book just reminds me of how anti capitalism really is just capitalism.

Anyways, Darrow drinks some serious dumb bitch juice. Didn’t like any of the characters besides sevro and mustang, and the middle section especially was a massive slog to get past. The story didn’t really start getting good until the last quarter so kudos to that I suppose. He can write action sequences well. Everything in between those parts sucks. I found a great portion of the twists to be predictable and not all that surprising.
